About

Marta Minujín is an Argentine artist known for her innovative and interactive art installations. She has been a pioneer in the use of technology in art, creating immersive experiences that engage the viewer in unique ways. Her work often explores themes of communication, culture, and the relationship between art and society.

Minujín's most famous work is the "Parthenon of Books," a large-scale installation made entirely of books that was erected in various locations around the world. This work serves as a powerful statement about censorship and the importance of freedom of expression.

In addition to her installations, Minujín has also created a number of public art projects, including sculptures and murals. Her work is characterized by its vibrant colors, playful forms, and a sense of whimsy that invites viewers to engage with the art in a personal way.
